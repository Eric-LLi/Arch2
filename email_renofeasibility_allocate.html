Before your meet your architect
We’ve given your architect an overview of your discussion with us to help start the conversation when you meet. Your architect will be in touch with you over the next few days to arrange a suitable time to meet. If you haven’t heard from your architect within three days of this booking confirmation please call us.

Before you meet it’s a good idea to start pulling together some notes on the project. Think about the current issues or limitations of your home/site and create a wish list of the things you’d like to include or improve in your new home. Don’t forget to have a realistic indication of your timing and budget. Are you aware of any limitations or restrictions that apply to your property such as heritage, landscape or planning overlays or a covenant? If you have any drawings of your property make sure you have a copy available for your architect as they will save time if a measure-up is required. A Certificate of Title and associated site plan described thereby will be most helpful.

There are a lot of things to consider and this meeting is a great time to get a better understanding of the journey you are about to embark on. You’ll find helpful hints on our website to help you prepare for your meeting.

Your Archicentre Australia Design Report
This Design service is a preliminary feasibility study created to help you to decide how or whether to proceed with a project. Your architect will arrange a second meeting to take you through a preliminary plan based on your brief, an indication of costs and recommendations on the best way to proceed with your project. This is the starting point to help you set your plans in motion. If you proceed you’ll need further design development sketches to finalise the design, and working drawings and specifications, which are essential for council approval and construction. Your architect will talk you through the next stage so you’ll understand how the process unfolds. You can always visit our website for tips and advice along the way.
